Have a question?

What is considered excess baggage?

Excess baggage refers to luggage that exceeds the weight or size limits set by airlines or shipping companies. It often includes additional or oversized items that cannot be accommodated within standard baggage allowances.

How can I send excess baggage?

You can send excess baggage through specialised courier services, luggage shipping companies, or by booking additional baggage with your airline. Each option has different procedures and costs.

What are the costs associated with sending excess baggage?

Costs vary depending on the size, weight, destination, and service provider. It’s best to check with your chosen courier or airline for specific pricing and any additional fees.

Do I need to pack my excess baggage differently?

Yes, excess baggage should be securely packed to prevent damage during transit. Follow the packing guidelines provided by the courier or airline, and consider using sturdy luggage or shipping containers.

How do I measure and weigh my excess baggage?

Use a reliable scale to weigh your baggage and measure its dimensions using a tape measure. Ensure you provide accurate weight and size information to avoid additional charges or delays.

What documents are required for sending excess baggage?

Required documents typically include a shipping label, a waybill or receipt, and, for international shipments, customs declaration forms. Check with your courier or airline for specific documentation requirements.

Can I track my excess baggage shipment?

Yes, many courier and shipping companies offer tracking services for excess baggage. You’ll receive a tracking number to monitor the status and location of your shipment online.

What happens if my excess baggage is lost or damaged?

Contact the courier or airline’s customer service immediately to report the issue. They will guide you through the claims process, which may involve providing proof of value and shipping details.

Are there any restrictions on what I can send as excess baggage?

Yes, restrictions vary by provider and destination. Commonly restricted items include hazardous materials, perishable goods, and certain electronics. Check with your courier or airline for specific guidelines.

How far in advance should I book excess baggage shipping?

It’s advisable to book excess baggage shipping as early as possible to ensure availability and secure the best rates. Some services require advance booking, especially during peak travel seasons.
